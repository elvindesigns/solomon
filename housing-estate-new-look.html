DONE AND DUSTED! World Bank Housing Estate Now Wearing A New Look!!

As the two weeks Roads Rehabilitation Exercise of World Bank Housing Estate comes to an end, I took time today to traverse through the nooks and crannies of the Estate to appraise the impact of the the exercise on the people of the area.

I discovered that the hilly and deep seated portholes that adorned the roads network - which made common trekking so difficult, let alone vehicular movement, have all disappeared and the entire Estate is now wearing a new look. Most of the sections that had almost turned to slumps due to flood in the past are now beehive of social activities and smooth vehicular movement.

The Machine Operators are to be commended for their professionalism in Grading the roads as most residents have started scooping/ distilling blocked drainage channels to ensure the durability of the exercise, having suffered from decades of neglect and abandonment in rehabilitating the roads by past Administrations. I also appreciate the effort of Chief Onyekwere Azubui√±e who ensured that no major road (including adjoining ones) was left unattended.

Today one can drive from one end of the Estate to the other unhindered as all the roads are now accessible. The roads include :-

  • Nwagbaoso road (through Ahia Nkwo, Abayi)
  • Christ Church Road

  • Abazie Street, etc

True to his words, Hon Solomon Adaelu has ensured that his Constituents at the grassroot are the direct beneficiaries of his Representation at the National Assembly; which is a departure from the old order - where few individuals (Politicians) had requested for the sharing of the Equipment fund amongst themselves, as a 'Stomach Infrastructure' Project. I wonder what would have been the benefit of our Constituents if such fund was earlier shared. People can now move freely in and around the Estate this Yuletide season as they celebrate with their loved ones.

Thank you the Messenger for this good Message to your Constituents in World Bank Housing Estate! Indeed the livelihood of the inhabitants have drastically improved with Children running around the Streets in celebration of their new/smooth roads and good environment. December 21, 2018
