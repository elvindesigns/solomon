HON SOLOMON ADAELU'S RECOMMENDATIONS ON POLICE BRUTALITY GETS SENATE'S ACCEPTANCE IN POLICE REFORM BILL

The Recommendations of the Motion by Hon Solomon Adaelu on Police Brutality on defenceless and hapless Citizens of the Nation ably moved in 2015 have been adopted in Police Reform Bill passed by the Senate. Most of the recommendations that are predicted mostly on the adherence to Rules of Engagement by the Nigerian Police Force while dealing with the Civilians were given express approval.

This Bill that came on the heel of the recent escalation in Extra Judicial killings by the Police Force especially the SARS unit is targeted to make the Nigerian Police Force to be accountable and take responsibility for their actions while engaging with the Public as canvassed by the Voice of the Voiceless in his Motion.

It is instructive to note that Hon Adaelu started this campaign when some of his Constituents were killed at Ariaria Market in 2015 by Officers of Nigerian Drug Law Enforcement Agency. He opined that Police and other Law enforcement agencies should respect the sanctity of life in their day to day operations. Most of the killings that take place are senseless and he wondered why any Security Personnel will take pleasure in the killing at every little 'provocation'. Sadly these Security Agencies ironically Kill and Brutalize the same people they are Constitutionally assigned to protect; and most of the killers go free without probe even when properly identified - thereby giving others the audacity to follow the trend as a norm.

It is believed that if the Police Force and other Security Agencies - like the Military, Customs, Nigeria Immigrations, NDLEA, Nigeria Civil Defense etc are made to be accountable for any death arising from their daily activities with the public they will sit up in their Responsibilities and these wanton and senseless killings and brutality that have heightened the level of insecurity in the Nation will reduce drastically.

However, it is disheartening that in this period of our National Development that a lot of people have died on our highways for their refusal to offer bribe as low as #50 to the Police or any other Security Agency. This eloquently testify how cheap the Citizens life have become in Nigeria.

The recent killing of a lady and brutalisation of her boyfriend in Lagos by the (F)SARS unit of the Police Force is a typical example of what Nigerians go through daily at the hands of the Security Agencies.

The Amnesty International has seriously condemned this ugly trend that has befallen Nigeria in their several Reports on Human Rights in the Country. Nigeria has become a flash point in Africa where life has become short and brutish due to unprofessional conduct of our Security Agencies while on duty.

We appreciate the Messenger for speaking out for the people and leading this Campaign to sanitize the Police Force and other Security Agencies - that wave off the senseless killings we see daily as 'Accidental Discharge'. This trend must change!
